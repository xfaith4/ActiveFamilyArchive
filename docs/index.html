What you can do inside

Twelve features working together to make family history approachable, searchable, and shareable.

🔍

Search & Profiles

Find anyone in the archive by name, year, or place. Each profile brings together life events, family relationships, photos, and family-authored notes.

  • Search by name, year, or place
  • Life events, birth and death details
  • Parents, spouse, children, and linked photos
  • Jump from one relative to another with a tap
🌳

Interactive Family Tree

Explore the family as an interactive diagram with bloodline, household, explore, and a me-centered relationship lens that always shows your own place in the tree.

  • Family, pedigree, descendants, and branch views
  • Me-centered Relationship Lens anchored to you
  • Selected people trace visibly back to you
  • Pan, zoom, and re-center if you get lost
💞

Relationship Labels & Trace

See exactly how any person is related to you — parent, aunt, second cousin, and beyond — in search results, profiles, and the family tree.

  • Kinship labels relative to your linked profile
  • Visible relationship trace in the tree
  • Info popover explains how each label is computed
  • Shown across search, profiles, and tree context
📷

Photo Gallery

Browse hundreds of historical family photos and documents across generations. Open any image in the in-app lightbox to read captions and hop to linked relatives.

  • 685+ scanned photos and documents
  • In-app lightbox with previous and next navigation
  • Person chips link directly to family profiles
  • Relatives can upload new photos for review
📚

Cited Sources Library

Every fact is anchored to a real record. The sources library keeps exact citations visible while making them easy to browse for non-technical family members.

  • 100 cited records from the family archive
  • Filter by record type, place, year range, or link
  • Related surnames, places, and linked relatives
  • Open the original citation or external record
🧭

Research Library & Guided Pathways

A friendlier research surface that groups record categories and helps relatives understand where to look next — no genealogy experience needed.

  • Record categories grouped by type
  • Guided pathways for common research questions
  • Plain-language explanations of each record type
  • Jump from a source to the people it touches
📍

Place Pages & Branch Guidance

Explore the recurring towns, counties, and family clusters that shape our story. Location pages connect people, sources, and research ideas.

  • Pages for recurring places in the archive
  • People and sources connected to each place
  • Branch-level research guidance
  • Geographic clustering around family hubs
📎

Evidence Attachments

Attach certificates, obituaries, family Bible pages, census scans, and other evidence directly to a person's profile — up to 15 MB per artifact.

  • Images or PDFs up to 15 MB each
  • Attached directly to the person they concern
  • Admin-reviewed before appearing in the archive
  • Supports birth, marriage, death, and obituary records
📊

Family Analytics

Discover patterns in the archive through family-wide summaries that stay grounded in real records and can be explored in more detail with one tap.

  • Surnames, given names, and birth decades
  • Living and deceased distribution
  • Geographic clustering and origin counts
  • Click any stat for a filtered people list
📈

Your Accuracy Score

See at a glance how complete and well-sourced the information is for the people closest to you, and where a little added context could strengthen the record.

  • Per-user data accuracy score
  • Nationality ratio based on your lineage
  • Highlights records that could use a source
  • Encourages careful, shared improvement
🛠️

Family Contributions

Relatives can help improve the archive without putting core genealogical facts at risk. Every contribution stays reviewable and traceable.

  • Suggest factual corrections with optional citations
  • Upload photos from a person's profile
  • Edit nicknames and profile notes with audit history
  • All contributions stay invitation-only and secure

Getting started

How to get access

Access is invitation-only to keep family data private. Here's how to get set up:

1
Request an invitation Contact the archive admin (see the Contact section below) and ask to be added. You'll need a Google account.
2
Sign in with Google Once you're added, open the archive and click "Sign in with Google." Use the same Google account you shared with the admin.
3
Start exploring Search for a relative, open their profile, browse the photo gallery, walk the family tree, or dip into the cited sources.

Tips for getting around

  • The search bar at the top searches every family member by name — the fastest way to find someone.
  • Inside a profile, tap any relative's name to hop straight to them.
  • In the family tree, scroll to zoom and click-drag to pan. Press the re-center button if you get lost.
  • The Relationship Lens keeps you at the center so you can see how everyone connects back to you.
  • The Cited Sources library explains each record type in plain language and ties sources back to people and places.
  • The Analytics page shows family-wide stats — tap any bar to open a filtered list of matching members.
  • To add a photo, open a profile and tap Add Photo. Admins review new uploads before they appear.
  • Spot something wrong? Use Suggest a Change inside the app or contact the admin below.
