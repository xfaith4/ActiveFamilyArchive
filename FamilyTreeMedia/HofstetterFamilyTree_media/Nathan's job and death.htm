Besides being a farmer Nathan was a surveyor for the United States Land Office. He made 15 trips to Il. surveying and adjusting property boundary disputes. On his 16th trip, march 20, 1854 he took his wife and children. They settled in Rock Island, Il., Nathan and daughter Rachel took sick from and epidemic of illness that struck several members of their community. Nathan was somehow given the wrong medicine and died shortly after. His daughter Rachel died 3 hrs. later.
